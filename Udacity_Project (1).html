DataSet used : No-show appointments¶

(original source on Kaggle)

Brief Introduction¶

The dataset used for our case study was loaded into our notebook as well as the packages which will be used to carry out our analysis. For this analysis, suitable packages are Pandas, Numpy and Matplotlib. After having a quick overview of the dataset, the following tentative questions arose which i assumed could be important to answer or start off with as we investigate the dataset.

Insightful Questions.¶

1- Could benefiting from a scholarship impact a patient's response to his/her appoinment? i.e Did patients who benefited from a scholarship show up more than those who didn't ?

2- Did the proportion of patients who received an SMS reminder honour their appointments more than the proportion who didn't?

3- What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

#Recall we created a list of patient types under the statistics section

#we create an array with the help of a for loop that will store:
#A) - the count for each disease
P_numbers = []

P_show_up = []  

#C) - the count of patients that have a scholarship ONLY, for each disease type
P_schol = []

#E) - the count of patients that show up for appoinments and have a scholarship ONLY, for each disease type
P_schol_show_up = []

P_schol_sms = []
P_schol_sms_show_up = []


for p in P_types:
    illness = df[df[p]==1]
    ill_count = illness.count()[p]
    P_numbers.append(ill_count)
    print("Type of illness :",p)
    print("Number of Patients :",ill_count)

    ill_showup = illness.query('No_show=="No"')

    print("Number of Patients who showed up for appoinments :",ill_show_count)

    ill_show_sms_count = ill_show_sms.count()[p] 
    P_sms_show_up.append(ill_show_sms_count)
    print("Number of Patients who showed up after receiving an sms only:",ill_show_sms_count)
    
    
#what proportion of patients with each disease benefit from a scholarship without receiving an sms
    ill_schol = illness.query('Scholarship == 1').query('SMS_received==0')
    ill_schol_count = ill_schol.count()[p]
    P_schol.append(ill_schol_count)
    print("Number of patients having a scholarship: ",ill_schol_count)
    
    #what proportion now show up for appoinments
    ill_schol_show = ill_schol.query('No_show=="No"')
    ill_schol_show_count = ill_schol_show.count()[p]
    P_schol_show_up.append(ill_schol_show_count)
    print("Number of patients having a scholarship only and who showed up :",ill_schol_show_count)
    
#Now what proportion of patients benefit from a scholarship and receive sms
    schol_sms = illness.query('Scholarship == 1').query('SMS_received==1')
    schol_sms_count = schol_sms.count()[p]
    P_schol_sms.append(schol_sms_count)
    print("Number of patients having a scholarship and an sms:",schol_sms_count)

    P_schol_sms_show_up.append(schol_sms_showup_count)

Conclusion¶

Using exploratory analysis. a tentative conclusion we can give is, though receiving an sms and getting a scholarship could be factors that influence a patient showing up for their appointment, patients who have a scholarship are more likely to show up for appointments that patients who only receive an sms for a reminder.
